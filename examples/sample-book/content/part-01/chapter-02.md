# Chapter 2 — The Teaching Begins

[[char:krishna|Krishna]] does not argue with [[char:arjuna|Arjuna]]'s grief; he widens the frame.
The body changes — childhood, youth, age — yet the one who watches remains.

Here the first key of [[term:dharma|dharma]] is offered: act because the action is right,
not because its result is sweet. This answers the doubt raised under
[the weight of the bow](../chapter-01.md#the-weight-of-the-bow).

## The Three Gates of Action (GFM showcase)

The teaching contrasts three stances toward the fruit of action:

| Stance | Motive | Outcome |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Attachment | Craves the result | Bondage |
| Rejection | Flees all action | Stagnation |
| Karma-yoga | Acts for the right | ~~Bondage~~ Freedom |

Arjuna's practice checklist as the chapter closes:

- [x] See the deathless self behind the changing body
- [x] Accept that action cannot be wholly renounced
- [ ] Act without grasping at the result
- [ ] Hold steadiness in pleasure and pain alike
