## A thought, written as a sutra

The discipline of [[term:dharma|dharma]] can be read as a formula: act, but let the
worth of the deed stay independent of its fruit — inline, that is $W(a) \perp F(a)$.

Set as a display formula, *nishkama karma* becomes:

$$
\text{freedom} = \text{action} - \lambda \cdot \text{attachment}(\text{fruit})
$$

As $\lambda \to 0$ the mind clings to outcomes; as $\lambda \to \infty$ the deed is
offered freely.
